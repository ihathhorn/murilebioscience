GHK-CU: The Copper Peptide with Four Decades of Skin Science Behind It

May 2025 · 5 min read · Murilé BioScience Research Blog

GHK-CU (copper peptide) is one of the most extensively researched peptides in dermatology. With over 40 years of published studies and a 2023 clinical trial showing measurable increases in skin collagen density, here is what the science says.

What is GHK-CU and Where Does It Come From?

GHK-CU is a naturally occurring tripeptide — glycine-histidine-lysine — bound to a copper ion. It was first identified in human plasma in 1973 by researcher Loren Pickart, who discovered it had remarkable tissue-regenerating properties. GHK-CU concentrations in your blood are naturally high in youth and decline significantly with age — a pattern that mirrors the visible decline in skin quality we associate with growing older.

The peptide affects approximately 31.2% of all human genes by either activating or deactivating them — working through what researchers describe as epigenetic mechanisms, meaning it changes which genes are switched on or off in skin cells.

What the Clinical Evidence Shows

A 2023 clinical trial published by Yuvan Research Inc., reported by EurekAlert, used high-resolution dermal ultrasound to measure skin collagen density before and after 3 months of GHK-CU gel use. Ultrasound imaging — the same technology used in medical diagnostics — showed a measurable increase in dermal collagen density in treated volunteers.

A separate 2023 double-blind, split-face study involving 60 participants aged 40-65 compared a 0.05% GHK-CU serum against placebo over 12 weeks. Results showed a 22% increase in skin firmness and a 16% reduction in fine lines measured by optical profilometry, with proteomic analysis confirming upregulation of collagen type I and decorin — proteins central to dermal structure.

Earlier Landmark StudyIn a trial by Abdulghani et al. (1999), topical GHK-CU outperformed both Vitamin C cream and retinoic acid — increasing collagen in 70% of volunteers versus 50% for Vitamin C and 40% for retinoic acid.

A 2025 comprehensive review covering studies from 2016-2025 (published in Medical Science Monitor) confirmed GHK-CU can penetrate the stratum corneum, where it helps reverse skin thinning, tighten loose tissue, repair the skin barrier, strengthen dermal structure, and reduce wrinkle depth and fine lines.

The Mechanism: How It Regenerates Skin

GHK-CU works through several interconnected pathways. It stimulates fibroblasts (the cells responsible for collagen and elastin production) to increase their activity. It modulates metalloproteinases — enzymes that break down collagen — keeping them in balance rather than allowing unchecked degradation. And it regulates over 1,500 genes involved in skin health, including growth factors that drive skin renewal.

Unlike topical retinoids, which work primarily on the surface, GHK-CU's subcutaneous administration delivers the peptide directly to the dermis where collagen is actually synthesised — bypassing the skin barrier entirely.

Adding Snap-8 targets expression lines specifically.
