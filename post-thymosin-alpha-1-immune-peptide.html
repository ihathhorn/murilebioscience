Thymosin Alpha-1: The Immune Peptide Approved in 35+ Countries

January 2025 · 6 min read · Murilé BioScience Research Blog

While many peptides remain in early research stages, Thymosin Alpha-1 has one of the deepest clinical evidence bases in the field — over 30 trials involving more than 11,000 human subjects. Here is what makes it unique.

What is Thymosin Alpha-1?

Thymosin Alpha-1 (Tα1) is a naturally occurring 28-amino acid peptide originally isolated from the thymus gland in the 1970s by researcher Allan Goldstein. The thymus is the organ responsible for producing and educating T-cells — your immune system's primary defence against infections, cancer cells, and other threats. Thymosin Alpha-1 is one of the key signalling molecules the thymus uses to regulate immune activity.

The synthetic form, thymalfasin (commercially known as Zadaxin), is approved in more than 35 countries for the treatment of hepatitis B, hepatitis C, and as an immune adjunct in cancer therapy. It is one of only a few peptides with this level of regulatory approval globally — giving it a clinical evidence base that stands apart from most research-grade peptides.

The Clinical Evidence Base: 11,000+ Human Subjects

A 2024 comprehensive narrative review published in Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine assessed the safety and efficacy of Thymosin Alpha-1 across more than 30 clinical trials involving over 11,000 human subjects. The review covered applications in COVID-19, infectious diseases, autoimmune conditions, and cancer treatment.

A 2024 updated meta-analysis of 12 sepsis randomised controlled trials (involving 1,894 patients) found that Tα1 reduced 28-day all-cause mortality by approximately 25% compared to control groups. The greatest benefit was seen in patients with the most severe immunoparalysis — those whose immune systems were most suppressed — suggesting Tα1 works specifically by restoring immune function rather than broadly stimulating it.

COVID-19 ResearchDuring the 2020 pandemic, Italian physicians began using Thymosin Alpha-1 in critically ill COVID-19 patients. The peptide's ability to repair immune dysregulation — including the dangerous overactivation of T-cells seen in severe COVID — generated significant research interest and several clinical investigations.

How It Modulates (Not Just Boosts) the Immune System

What makes Thymosin Alpha-1 particularly notable is its bidirectional immune modulation. Unlike supplements that broadly "boost" immune activity — which can be dangerous if the immune system is already overactive — Tα1 enhances suppressed immunity while simultaneously helping to dampen hyperinflammation.

Mechanistically, Tα1 activates dendritic cells and T-lymphocytes, increases cytokine production (the chemical messengers that coordinate immune responses), enhances the body's response to vaccines and infections, and promotes clearance of infected or damaged cells. A 2024 review also noted its potential in supporting autoimmune conditions including rheumatoid arthritis and multiple sclerosis, though the clinical evidence in this area is less developed than for infectious disease.

Practical Wellness Applications

For general immune wellness, Thymosin Alpha-1 is typically used in cycles — two to three injections per week for four to eight weeks — particularly during seasonal illness periods, periods of high stress (which significantly suppress immune function), recovery from illness, and preventive support during travel.

Its safety profile is well-established across decades of clinical use: well-tolerated with only minor side effects reported across thousands of trial participants. This safety record, combined with its deep evidence base, makes it one of the most reassuring immune peptides available.

⚕ Regulatory Note: While approved in 35+ countries, regulatory status varies by jurisdiction. In the Philippines, peptides for wellness use fall under general supplement guidelines. Always consult a healthcare professional before beginning any immune peptide protocol, particularly if you have existing immune conditions or are taking immunosuppressive medications.
